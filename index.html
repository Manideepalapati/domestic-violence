What Is Domestic Violence?

Domestic violence is a pattern of abusive behavior in any relationship that is used by one partner to gain or maintain power and control over another intimate partner. Domestic violence can be physical, sexual, emotional, economic, psychological, or technological actions or threats of actions or other patterns of coercive behavior that influence another person within an intimate partner relationship. This includes any behaviors that intimidate, manipulate, humiliate, isolate, frighten, terrorize, coerce, threaten, blame, hurt, injure, or wound someone.

Types of Abuse

physical Abuse

This occurs when someone physically contacts someone in an unwanted way with the intention of causing them harm

Emotional abuse

This occurs when someone threatens,insults,humiliates,intimidates,isolates or excessively monitors someone

Sexual Abuse

This occurs when someone pressures or coerces someone to do something sexually that they don't want to.

Financial Abuse

This occurs when someone attempts to control someones monetary actions and decisions.

Stalking

This occurs when someone watches,follows, or harasses someone repeatedly.

Digital Abuse

This ccurs when someone uses technology to bully,harass,stalk, or intimidate someone.
